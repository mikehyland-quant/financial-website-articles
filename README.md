# Published Articles – Derivatives Market Structure & Product Innovation

This repository contains three published industry articles I wrote focused on interest rate derivatives, futures product design, and swap market structure innovation.

The articles were originally published on TABB Forum and discuss portfolio compression, futures contract construction, and structural inefficiencies in swap markets.

---

## 1. Exchanges Should Disrupt Swap Portfolio Compression Using Single-Sided Swap Futures  
Published: May 2016  

PDF: [Download Article](./exchanges-should-disrupt-swap-portfolio-compression-using-single-sided-swap-futures.pdf)

This article explores how futures exchanges could meaningfully disrupt OTC swap portfolio compression by introducing more flexible swap futures products.

Key themes:
- OTC swap compression mechanics
- Exchange for Physical (EFP) as a compression mechanism
- Structural limitations of traditional swap futures
- Introduction of Single-Sided Swap Futures (SSSF)
- Clearing and netting efficiency

The central argument: separating floating and fixed legs into independent futures contracts enables efficient OTC compression and improves market transparency.

## 2. Interest Rate Swap Futures à la Carte – Using Single-Sided Swap Futures  
Published: July 2016  

PDF:  
→ [Download Article](./docs/interest-rate-swap-futures-a-la-carte-using-single-sided-swap-futures.pdf)

This article proposes a “deconstructed menu” approach to swap futures design.

Rather than bundling fixed and floating legs into a single instrument, the framework allows participants to build swaps from individual components.

Highlights:
- Product flexibility via separate floating and fixed contracts
- Improved transparency vs current swap futures
- Corporate and bank hedging use cases
- Intra-product collateral offsets
- Enhanced clearing efficiency

The article argues that modular contract design can attract both buy-side and dealer participation while expanding exchange share of OTC flows.
